## BRAND POSITIONING

### Positioning Statement Formula
> For [target customer] who [need/pain], [Product] is a [category] that [key benefit]. Unlike [alternatives], we [differentiator].

### April Dunford's Obviously Awesome (5 Components)
1. **Competitive Alternatives** -- What would customers use if you didn't exist?
2. **Unique Attributes** -- Features/capabilities only you have
3. **Value** -- Benefits those attributes enable
4. **Target Customers** -- Who cares most about that value
5. **Market Category** -- Context that makes your value obvious

Positioning Canvas:
> We are the only [category] that [unique attribute] for [target customer] who [key pain point] unlike [alternatives] we [key differentiator].

### StoryBrand Framework (Donald Miller) -- 7 Elements
1. Character (customer is hero), 2. Problem (external/internal/philosophical), 3. Guide (your brand: empathy + authority), 4. Plan (3-step process), 5. Call to Action, 6. Success (life after), 7. Failure (stakes).

One-Liner: > We help [target customer] [solve problem] so they can [achieve result].

### Value Proposition Canvas
Customer Profile: Jobs-to-be-done, Pains, Gains --> Value Map: Products/Services, Pain Relievers, Gain Creators.

### Brand Voice Dimensions (4D Model)

| Dimension | Spectrum |
|-----------|----------|
| Character | Institutional <-> Personal |
| Tone | Formal <-> Casual |
| Language | Complex <-> Simple |
| Purpose | Inform <-> Entertain |

Define 3-4 voice attributes with Do/Don't for each. Document with examples across contexts: headlines, body copy, CTAs, errors, emails, social, push notifications.

### Voice Archetypes by Company Type
- Dev tools: Technical, direct, no fluff (Stripe, Vercel, Linear)
- Consumer: Friendly, emotional, relatable (Airbnb, Duolingo, Spotify)
- B2B SaaS: Professional, helpful, clear (Salesforce, HubSpot, Slack)
- Fintech: Trustworthy, simple, reassuring (Wise, Ramp, Mercury)

### Messaging Architecture (Hierarchy)
Brand Promise (unchanging) --> Value Propositions (by audience) --> Key Messages (by product/feature) --> Proof Points (evidence). Create audience-specific messaging tables: Persona | Pain Point | Value Prop | Key Message.

### Positioning Mistakes to Flag
1. Positioning on features not outcomes
2. Too broad (appeals to everyone = no one)
3. Undifferentiated (could apply to any competitor)
4. Inside-out (what you want to say vs. what customers hear)
5. Static (never revisiting as market evolves)

## CONTENT STRATEGY

### Content Pillar Model
Choose 3-5 pillars connecting expertise to customer problems. Pillar types: Product-adjacent, Industry trends, Customer success, Thought leadership.

Selection criteria (score 1-5 each): Search volume, Business relevance, Expertise/authority, Competition, Evergreen potential.

### Pillar-Cluster Model (HubSpot)
Pillar Page (3,000-5,000 words) --> 4-6 Cluster Posts (1,500-2,500 words each) --> All interlinked. Signals topic authority, creates internal linking, captures long-tail keywords.

### Content Moat Types
- Data moat: Proprietary data only you have (very high defensibility)
- Expertise moat: Deep domain knowledge (high)
- Volume moat: Comprehensive coverage (medium)
- Community moat: User-generated content (very high)
- Brand moat: Trusted name = clicks (high)

### 70-20-10 Content Mix
- 70% Core: SEO-driven, pillar/cluster, proven topics
- 20% Thought leadership: Original POV, trends, commentary
- 10% Experimental: New formats, risky takes, viral attempts

### Jobs-to-be-Done Content Framework
- Learn: Educational ("What is [topic]?" guides)
- Compare: Comparison ("[Tool A] vs [Tool B]")
- Do: How-to ("How to [accomplish goal]")
- Validate: Social proof (case studies, testimonials)
- Buy: Bottom-funnel (pricing, demos, trials)

### Content Format Selection by Stage
- Early (0-1K): Long-form blog, Twitter/LinkedIn threads. 2-4 posts/month.
- Growth (1K-10K): + Newsletter, templates/tools. 4-8 posts/month.
- Scale (10K+): + Podcast, video, webinars. 8-16 posts/month.

### Blog Post Types
| Type | Word Count | SEO Value |
|------|------------|-----------|
| Pillar/Ultimate Guide | 3,000-7,000 | Very high |
| How-to | 1,500-2,500 | High |
| Listicle | 1,500-3,000 | Medium-high |
| Thought leadership | 1,000-2,000 | Low (brand value) |
| News/commentary | 500-1,000 | Low |
| Case study | 1,500-2,500 | Medium |

### 80/20 Distribution Rule
Spend 20% creating, 80% distributing. 1 pillar piece --> 10+ distribution pieces.

Distribution channels: Owned (blog, newsletter) | Social (Twitter, LinkedIn) | Communities (Reddit, Slack, Discord) | Syndication (Medium, Dev.to, HN).

### 1:10 Repurposing Workflow
Blog post --> Twitter thread, LinkedIn post, newsletter section, Reddit comment, Quora answer, slide deck, short video, infographic, podcast talking points, community discussion starter.

Distribution timing: Day 0 (blog + email + Twitter + LinkedIn), Day 1-3 (groups + Reddit + HN), Day 1-7 (Slack communities), Week 1-2 (Quora), Week 2+ (syndication).

### Platform-Specific Tactics
- Twitter: Thread format best, hook first tweet, CTA last, post 8-10AM or 7-9PM
- LinkedIn: Native posts > links, first line is hook, 1-3 hashtags, document posts work
- Reddit: 10:1 ratio (helpful comments per share), be human not brand, subreddit-specific
- Hacker News: "Show HN" for launches, original research performs, no marketing-speak

### Content Metrics
| Goal | Primary | Secondary |
|------|---------|-----------|
| Awareness | Pageviews, uniques, social reach | Time on page, bounce rate |
| Engagement | Comments, shares, email signups | Scroll depth, return visits |
| SEO | Rankings, organic traffic, backlinks | DA, indexed pages |
| Conversion | Signups, MQLs, pipeline influenced | Assisted conversions |

Newsletter benchmarks: Open rate 25%+ (good), 35%+ (great), 45%+ (excellent). Click rate 3%+ / 5%+ / 8%+.

### Content Mistakes to Flag
No distribution plan, too many channels, vanity metrics, no SEO foundation, no content updates, too many pillars, no internal linking, gating everything, no repurposing.

## SEO STRATEGY

### Ranking Formula
Ranking = Relevance x Authority x User Experience. Relevance = intent match. Authority = backlinks + domain strength + E-E-A-T. UX = speed + mobile + engagement.

### E-E-A-T Signals
Experience (first-hand insights), Expertise (author credentials, depth), Authoritativeness (backlinks, citations, mentions), Trustworthiness (HTTPS, accuracy, ownership).

### Search Intent Types
| Intent | Content Type | Example |
|--------|-------------|---------|
| Informational | Blog, guide, video | "how to start a podcast" |
| Navigational | Homepage, brand page | "spotify login" |
| Commercial | Comparison, review | "best crm software" |
| Transactional | Product page, signup | "buy airpods pro" |

### Keyword Research Framework
Keyword types by funnel:
- Problem-aware (informational, top): "how to manage remote team"
- Solution-aware (comparison, middle): "best project management software"
- Product-aware (transactional, bottom): "asana vs monday"
- Brand (navigational, bottom): "[brand] pricing"

Evaluation factors: Volume, Difficulty, Intent match, Business value.

### Keyword Difficulty Tiers
- Low (0-20): Low DA sites, thin content --> Start here
- Medium (20-50): Mixed competition --> After early wins
- High (50-70): Established sites --> With authority
- Very High (70+): Major brands --> Long-term goal

Long-tail strategy: Lower competition, higher intent, faster wins. Find via Google Autocomplete, People Also Ask, Related Searches, Answer the Public, Reddit/Quora.

### On-Page SEO Checklist (Priority Order)
1. Title tag: Primary keyword + hook, <60 chars (Critical)
2. H1: One per page, includes primary keyword (Critical)
3. Meta description: Keyword + CTA, <155 chars (High)
4. URL: Short, keyword, hyphens (High)
5. First 100 words: Include primary keyword naturally (High)
6. Internal links: Descriptive anchor text (High)
7. H2-H6: Secondary keywords where natural (Medium)
8. Image alt text: Descriptive with keyword (Medium)
9. External links: Authoritative sources (Medium)

### Technical SEO Checklist
HTTPS, mobile-friendly, site speed (LCP <2.5s, INP <200ms, CLS <0.1), indexability, XML sitemap, robots.txt, canonical tags, structured data. Flat architecture: every page within 3 clicks of homepage.

### Internal Linking Strategy
Hub-and-spoke: Pillar <-> Clusters, clusters <-> each other. Descriptive anchor text. Link from high-authority pages. Fix orphan pages.

### Link Building (Ranked by Effectiveness)
1. Original research/data (high effort, very high quality)
2. Expert roundups (medium, high)
3. Guest posting (medium, medium-high)
4. Broken link building (medium, medium, high scalability)
5. Resource page outreach (low, medium, high scalability)
6. HARO/journalist requests (low, varies)
7. Podcast appearances (medium, medium)
8. Competitor backlink analysis (medium, varies, high scalability)

Healthy anchor distribution: Branded 40-50%, Naked URL 20-30%, Generic 10-20%, Keyword-rich 5-15%.

### 90-Day SEO Plan
Month 1 (Foundation): Keyword research (50-100 KWs), competitive analysis, technical audit, fix critical issues, on-page optimization, first pillar page.
Month 2 (Content): 4 cluster posts linked to pillar, 1 linkable asset, internal linking audit.
Month 3 (Growth): 4 more posts, link building outreach (10-20 pitches), guest post/podcast outreach, review metrics.

### SEO Benchmarks by Stage
| Stage | Organic Traffic | Ranking KWs | Monthly Content |
|-------|----------------|-------------|-----------------|
| 0-6 months | 500-2,000 | 50-200 | 4-8 posts |
| 6-12 months | 2,000-10,000 | 200-1,000 | 8-12 posts |
| 12-24 months | 10,000-50,000 | 1,000-5,000 | 12-20 posts |
| 24+ months | 50,000+ | 5,000+ | 15-30 posts |

### SEO Tools Stack
Free essentials: Google Search Console, Google Analytics 4, Screaming Frog (500 URLs), PageSpeed Insights. Paid: Ahrefs/Semrush ($99-119/mo).

### SEO Mistakes to Flag
Targeting impossible keywords, ignoring search intent, thin content, no internal linking, duplicate content, slow site, chasing algorithm updates, buying links, keyword stuffing, not tracking.

## PR & MEDIA STRATEGY

### Newsworthiness Test (All must pass)
1. Why now? (timeliness)
2. Why should readers care? (relevance to audience)
3. What's the conflict/tension? (narrative)
4. What's unique? (not "another startup")
5. Can you prove it? (data/evidence)

### When PR Makes Sense
High-impact: Funding ($5M+ or notable investors), major product launch, significant milestone, original research/data, acquisition, counter-narrative.
Bad time: No news hook, early MVP, "we exist" story, copycat product, no media relationships.

### PR Tier Strategy

**Tier 1 (Major Tech/Business Press):** TechCrunch, The Information, WSJ, NYT, Bloomberg, Forbes, Wired. Approach: Exclusive offer, strong hook. Lead time: 2-4 weeks.

**Tier 2 (Industry/Vertical Press):** SaaStr, Crunchbase News, vertical-specific publications. Approach: Targeted pitch. Lead time: 1-2 weeks.

**Tier 3 (Blogs, Newsletters, Podcasts):** TLDR, Morning Brew, industry newsletters, influential blogs. Approach: Direct outreach, easier access. Lead time: 1 week.

### Pitch Structure
```
Subject: [Specific hook] -- [Company] [achievement/news]
P1: THE NEWS (who, what, when, why it matters) -- 2-3 sentences
P2: CONTEXT (why now, market trend, problem solved) -- 2-3 sentences
P3: PROOF POINTS (traction, customers, investors) -- 2-3 sentences
P4: THE ASK (exclusive, interview, demo, assets available) -- 1-2 sentences
Signature: Name, title, one-line company desc, contact
```

### Subject Line Formulas (What Works)
- [Company] + [Achievement]: "Notion hits 30M users"
- Exclusive: [News]: "Exclusive: [Company] raises $50M"
- [Counter-narrative]: "Why [Company] isn't raising VC"
- [Data point]: "[Company] data: 73% of devs now use AI"
- Quick question: "Quick Q re: your AI coverage"

### Exclusive Strategy
Major funding ($20M+): Tier 1 exclusive, 1-2 week window. Timeline: Week -2 offer exclusive --> Week -1 share under embargo --> Day -1 final confirm --> Day 0 story live --> Day 0+ pitch Tier 2/3 referencing coverage.

### Follow-Up Sequence
Day 0: Full pitch. Day 3-4: Brief bump + new info. Day 7-8: Different angle. After 2 follow-ups: Move on.

### Press Kit Essentials
Founder photos (300dpi), product screenshots, logo files (SVG/PNG light+dark), company descriptions (25/50/100 word versions), founder bios (50+150 word), fact sheet, product video/GIF (30-60s), pre-approved customer quotes.

### Interview Prep Framework
Before any interview: 3 key messages (what MUST you communicate), proof points for each, anticipated tough questions with prepared answers, bridging phrases ("What's really important is..."), forbidden topics list.

### DIY vs. Agency Decision
DIY when: Early stage, budget-constrained, founder has media relationships, niche publications, 5+ hrs/week available.
Agency when: Major announcement, $10K+/month budget, need relationships fast, want ongoing strategy. Costs: Boutique $5-15K/mo, Mid-size $15-30K/mo, Large $30K+/mo.

### PR Metrics
Coverage volume, publication tier mix, share of voice vs. competitors, message pull-through (>50% target), referral traffic, domain authority improvement, social amplification.

### PR Mistakes to Flag
Pitching non-news, mass blasting same pitch, wrong beat/publication, bad timing (major news cycles), overpromising metrics, no follow-up, too much follow-up (>2), missing assets, embargo breaks, only chasing Tier 1.

## COMMUNITY BUILDING

### Community Models
- Product community: Users helping users (Notion, Figma)
- Professional community: Career/skill-focused (dbt, On Deck)
- Interest community: Shared passion (Indie Hackers)
- Customer community: Support + feedback (Salesforce Trailblazers)
- Creator community: Building in public (Substack)

### Community Value Proposition
> Join [Community Name] to [primary value] with [who's in it]. Members get [specific benefits] that help them [outcome].

Member motivations: Learning, Access (experts/founders), Networking, Support, Belonging, Recognition.

### Platform Selection Matrix (Score 1-5 each)
Factors: Audience fit, Engagement model (real-time vs async), Scale potential, Cost at scale, Control level, Integration with stack.

Recommendations by company type:
- B2B SaaS: Slack or Circle
- Dev Tools: Discord
- Consumer App: Discord or Geneva
- Enterprise: Salesforce Community or Khoros
- Course/Creator: Circle or Mighty Networks
- Open Source: Discord + GitHub Discussions

### Community Growth Stages

**Seed (0-100):** Quality > quantity. Hand-pick members, personal invites. Founder present daily. Establish norms. Find champions. Start with 4-6 channels max.

**Build (100-1,000):** Establish weekly/daily rituals. Add 1-2 volunteer moderators. Launch first champion program. Create FAQ/resource library.

**Grow (1,000-10,000):** Delegate to empowered moderators/champions. Segment into sub-communities. Regular events (virtual + IRL). Badges, levels, spotlights.

**Scale (10,000+):** Systematize with playbooks. Dedicated community team. Formal ambassador program with incentives. Multiple touchpoints (online + offline, global chapters).

### Engagement Tactics Calendar
| Tactic | Frequency | Purpose |
|--------|-----------|---------|
| Welcome messages | Every new member | Activation |
| Discussion prompts | 2-3x/week | Engagement |
| AMAs / Office hours | Weekly/biweekly | Value, access |
| Member spotlights | Weekly | Recognition |
| Wins thread | Weekly | Celebration |
| Exclusive content | Monthly | Retention |
| Events (virtual/IRL) | Monthly/quarterly | Connection |

### Onboarding Flow
Join --> Welcome DM (immediate) --> Prompted intro post (day 0-1) --> First value moment (day 1-3) --> First connection (day 3-7) --> First contribution (day 7-14).

### Ambassador Program Levels
Member (active participant) --> Contributor (regular contributions, badge) --> Champion (leadership, swag, founder access) --> Ambassador (external advocacy, compensation, events).

### Community Team Sizing
- 0-1K members: Founder + 1-2 volunteer mods
- 1K-5K: Part-time community manager
- 5K-20K: Full-time community manager
- 20K+: Community team (manager + coordinator)

### Community Health Metrics
| Metric | Healthy | Warning |
|--------|---------|---------|
| DAU/MAU | >20% | <10% |
| Posts per day | Growing MoM | Declining |
| Response time | <24 hours | >48 hours |
| Member-generated content | >50% | <20% |
| Monthly churn | <5% | >10% |
| Active members | >30% of total | <15% |

### Engagement Tiers
Lurkers (60-70%, activate), Casual (15-25%, increase), Active (5-15%, retain/recognize), Super users (1-5%, empower/reward).

### Community Mistakes to Flag
Building before PMF, wrong platform for audience, ghost town launch, no clear purpose, over-moderating, under-moderating, founder disappears, chasing member count over engagement, no recognition for super users, copying others blindly.

## CHANNEL PRIORITIZATION

### Channel Selection by Business Type
| Type | Primary | Secondary |
|------|---------|-----------|
| B2B SaaS | LinkedIn, SEO/blog, community | Twitter, newsletter, PR |
| Dev Tools | Twitter, GitHub, technical blog | Reddit, HN, Discord |
| Consumer | TikTok/Instagram, SEO, PR | Twitter, community, influencers |
| Marketplace | SEO, supply-side community | PR, local marketing |
| Fintech | SEO, trust content, PR | LinkedIn, partnerships |

### Channel Evaluation Framework (Score 1-5 each)
1. **Audience fit** -- Is your target customer here?
2. **Content fit** -- Does your content work on this channel?
3. **Competition** -- How saturated is it?
4. **Founder fit** -- Do you enjoy/excel at this channel?
5. **Resource fit** -- Can you sustain it with current resources?

**Focus Rule:** Master 1-2 channels before adding more. Excellent on 2 > mediocre on 5.

## HIRING DECISIONS

### When to Hire Marketing
Prerequisites: Founder has done marketing 6+ months, at least one channel working, brand foundation documented, can afford 12+ months salary, clear on what you need.

First hire by situation: Content working --> Content marketer. Need positioning --> Brand marketer (senior). SEO primary --> SEO specialist. Community core --> Community manager. Multiple channels --> Marketing generalist.

### When to Engage PR Agency
Consider at: Newsworthy milestones, $10K+/month budget, need media relationships, want ongoing strategy.
DIY when: Early stage, budget-constrained, founder has relationships, niche/trade targets.
